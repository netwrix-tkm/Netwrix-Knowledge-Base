How to : How To Join or Leave a Group on behalf of another User

Applies To:

Directory Manager 11

Business Scenario:

El is a Finance manager at Tyrell Corporation and would like his direct report, Abbey, to join a communication group named Fintech, so that he can remain updated about an upcoming project. The Fintech group, however, is managed by a different team/owner. Abbey can request to join the group on his own, but it would be much easier if El could just place a join�request on behalf of his subordinates. The idea of responsibly introducing a subordinate to a new working group by placing a join request on his or her behalf would also make the approval decision easy for the Fintech group owner.

Explanation:

The Directory Manager introduced the�group types�feature, where users can request to join or leave a�semi-private�group.

Directory Manager has a feature where managers and peers can generate a request on behalf of other users, and any user can place a request to join or leave a group on behalf of any user.

But before we move towards the actual steps for placing a request, here is a summary table for what we are about to do.
